Logo Design Project

This project was created to educate students on understanding the elements of logo design. For the project, they were instructed to conduct research on successful branding and icon desing. Students then used this information to create logos based on their own name; therefore, becoming the client and designer.

Uppercase Roman – New York.
Lowercase Roman – Helevtica and Minion Variable Concept.
Contrast of Form – The Bold Font.
Uppercase Italic – Didot.
Lowercase Italic – Source Code Variable.
Contrast of Form and Size – Didot.
Repition: Rotation, Radiation, Concentricity – Glitten Regular.
Pattern: Anomaly – New York and Champagne & Limousines.
Assimilated Element – Didot.
Group Orientation/Shape: 3D – Champagne & Limousines.

Stationary Project

The goal of this project was to learn about the elements of stationary and resume design. Students were to use previous logo designs as refernce for creating a letterhead which will be used on a resume, cover letter, and reference list. In addition to the documents, students are to create matching envelope and business card designs.

World Record Poster

Students were tasked with creating a poster to illustrate a world record. Each student was allowed to choose their own world Record from a list provided by the instructor. They were allowed free range in all aspects of the design; their only requirements being the use of the provided text with each record and the offical Word Record logo.

Neville Brody Poster

To further educate on the evolution of design, students were tasked with researching an assigned designer, creating an informational and well designed presentation, as well as an accompanying poster. The presentations and posters are meant to embody the style of each assinged designer, while still cleary being made and influenced by the student designers' personal style.
